Top Thanksgiving Recipes

Preheat the stove to 350 degrees F.

Dissolve the spread in a little pot. Include the get-up-and-go and squeeze of the lemon and 1 teaspoon of thyme leaves to the spread blend. Put in a safe spot.

Remove the giblets from the turkey and wash the turkey all around. Evacuate any abundance fat and extra pinfeathers and pat the outside dry. Spot the turkey in a huge simmering container. Generously salt and pepper within the turkey depression. Stuff the cavity with the bundle of thyme, divided lemon, quartered onion, and the garlic. Brush the outside of the turkey with the spread blend and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Tie the legs together with string and fold the wing tips under the body of the turkey.

Broil the turkey around 2 1/2 hours, or until the juices run clear when you cut between the leg and the thigh. Evacuate the turkey to a cutting board and spread with aluminum foil; let rest for 20 minutes.

Cut the turkey and serve.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 pound (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 1 lemon, zested and juiced
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 fresh turkey (10 to 12 pounds)
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 large bunch fresh thyme
  • 1 whole lemon, halved
  • 1 Spanish onion, quartered
  • 1 head garlic, halved crosswise
